# How Booking Works

Booking a doctor on MedBook always goes through the same steps, whether you
use the search page or chat with the assistant:

1. **Search** for a doctor by specialization, city, or fee.
2. **Pick a slot** — you'll see the exact fee and clinic address before
   anything is held.
3. **A hold (draft) is created** for 10 minutes. The fee and address you see
   at this moment are locked in for this booking, even if the doctor changes
   their listed fee later.
4. **You must tap Confirm** within those 10 minutes, or the hold expires and
   the slot becomes available to other patients again. Nothing is booked
   automatically — the assistant can only create this kind of hold, never a
   final confirmed appointment.
5. Once you confirm, the request goes to the doctor as **pending**. The
   doctor has up to 24 hours (or until 2 hours before the appointment,
   whichever comes first) to accept it.
6. When the doctor accepts, your appointment is **confirmed** and you'll get
   a reminder 24 hours and 1 hour before it starts.

Every patient profile can hold at most 3 active drafts at once, and only one
per doctor — this stops slots being hoarded by accident.
